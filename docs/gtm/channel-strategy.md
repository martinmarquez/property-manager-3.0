## 1. Top 3 Acquisition Channels (Ranked by CAC + Reach)

### Ranking Methodology
Channels scored on: estimated CAC (lower = better), reach in AR B2B SaaS real estate segment, speed to first conversion, and strategic fit with Tokko churn window.

---

### #1: Content SEO + Organic Search

**Estimated CAC:** $15–$40 USD  
**Reach:** High (long-term compounding)  
**Time to first conversion:** 3–6 months (requires patience)  
**Best for:** Solo + Agencia tiers

**Why it's #1:**  
Argentine real estate professionals actively search for software comparisons and how-tos. The "alternativa tokko broker" and "software inmobiliaria argentina" keyword clusters have real volume with low competition. A well-executed SEO strategy can capture high-intent buyers at near-zero marginal cost per visit.

**Execution:**
- Publish 2 long-form articles/month starting Week 20 (pre-launch)
- Anchor content: comparison pages, migration guides, feature explainers
- Target keyword clusters (see Section 2)
- Build topical authority around "gestión inmobiliaria Argentina" before GA

---

### #2: Association Partnerships (CUCICBA + CMAYDI)

**Estimated CAC:** $30–$80 USD  
**Reach:** Medium (concentrated, high-trust)  
**Time to first conversion:** 4–8 weeks after partnership activated  
**Best for:** Agencia + Pro tiers

**Why it's #2:**  
CUCICBA (Colegio Único de Corredores Inmobiliarios de la Ciudad de Buenos Aires) and CMAYDI (Cámara de Agentes Inmobiliarios y Desarrollistas) have direct access to licensed brokers and agencies. A newsletter feature or member discount carries institutional trust that no paid ad can replicate in this market.

**Execution:**
- Week 25: Outreach to CUCICBA communications director with co-marketing proposal
- Offer: exclusive member discount (15% off first year) + dedicated migration support
- Ask: newsletter feature, LinkedIn post, joint webinar
- CMAYDI: press release on launch + joint webinar for members at GA
- Expected yield: 50–100 high-quality agency leads per newsletter feature

---

### #3: Referral Program (Virality Engine)

**Estimated CAC:** $20–$60 USD  
**Reach:** Medium (grows with user base)  
**Time to first conversion:** Immediate once base exists (Week 28+)  
**Best for:** All tiers; strongest virality in Agencia cohort

**Why it's #3:**  
Argentine real estate is a relationship-driven market. Brokers know other brokers. A well-designed referral program with meaningful incentives creates compounding acquisition from the existing customer base. Low CAC since the primary cost is the referral incentive, not media spend.

**Mechanics:** (see Section 3)

## 3. Referral Program Design

### Mechanics

**Program name:** "Recomendá Corredor"  
**Trigger:** Available to any paid subscriber (all tiers)  
**Referral link:** Unique per account, shareable via email/WhatsApp

**Incentive Structure:**

| Action | Referrer reward | Referred reward |
|--------|----------------|-----------------|
| Referred signs up for trial | — | Full 14-day Pro trial (same as standard) |
| Referred converts to paid | 1 month free | 1 month at 30% off |
| Referred upgrades to Pro | 2 months free | 2 months at 30% off |

*Rewards credited to billing immediately on conversion confirmation.*

**Why this structure works:**
- Referrer incentive is purely success-based (no cost unless conversion happens)
- No referral fraud risk (can't self-refer, credit only on paid conversion)
- Both sides win — asymmetric design (referrer gets more) motivates advocacy

### K-Factor Projection

K-factor = invites per customer × conversion rate of invites

| Scenario | Invites/customer/mo | Invite → trial rate | Trial → paid rate | K-factor |
|----------|--------------------|--------------------|-------------------|----------|
| Conservative | 0.5 | 20% | 25% | 0.025 |
| Base case | 1.2 | 25% | 25% | 0.075 |
| Optimistic | 2.0 | 30% | 30% | 0.18 |

**Target K-factor: 0.08–0.12** — meaningful virality without being unrealistic for B2B SaaS.  
At K=0.10 with 100 paid customers, the referral program generates ~10 new customers/month organically.

### Activation Triggers
- Day 3 post-conversion: "¿Conocés a alguien que también gestione propiedades?" in-app modal
- Monthly billing email includes referral link in footer
- Agency dashboard: persistent "Invitá a un colega" banner

## 6. Tokko Churn Interception Strategy

### The Opportunity
Tokko Broker has known friction points: dated UI, opaque pricing, limited mobile experience, no AI features. Unhappy Tokko users are the highest-intent prospects for Corredor — they're already motivated to switch, they just need a credible destination.

### Interception Channels

**1. Search intent capture**
- Own "alternativa tokko broker" and "tokko precio" keywords in Google Ads
- Create dedicated SEO page: `/tokko-vs-corredor` with transparent feature/price comparison
- Target Tokko contract renewal windows (typically annual — April/October in AR)

**2. Content-driven interception**
- Blog series: "Por qué las inmobiliarias están migrando de Tokko"
- LinkedIn outreach to agency owners who mention Tokko frustration in posts
- YouTube: "Tutorial: migrá de Tokko a Corredor en 30 minutos"

**3. Migration offer mechanics**
- "Los primeros 100 en migrar de Tokko: 3 meses gratis en Agencia o Pro"
- One-click Tokko import tool (backed by [RENA-19](/RENA/issues/RENA-19)) removes the biggest friction point
- White-glove migration call for agencies 10+ users

**4. Community seeding**
- Monitor Reddit r/argentina, Grupos de Facebook de corredores inmobiliarios, WhatsApp groups for organic mentions of Tokko dissatisfaction
- Provide helpful, non-promotional responses — establish trust before pitching

**5. Direct outbound (Empresa tier)**
- Identify top 50 agencies in Buenos Aires by listings volume (ZonaProp public data)
- LinkedIn outreach from founder/CEO account — personalized, not templated
- Objective: 5–10 Empresa pipeline leads in first 6 months

## 7. Launch Funnel Metrics — Baseline Targets

### Definitions
- **Waitlist signup:** Email captured pre-launch (before trial available)
- **Trial start:** Account created, onboarding step 1 completed (first property added)
- **Trial-to-paid conversion:** Trial account upgrades to any paid plan within 30 days of trial end
- **MRR:** Monthly Recurring Revenue (USD)

### Targets by Phase

| Metric | Pre-launch (W20–27) | Beta (W28–31) | Month 1 post-GA | Month 6 post-GA |
|--------|---------------------|---------------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Waitlist signups/week | 15–25 | — | — | — |
| Total waitlist at beta | 150+ | — | — | — |
| Trial starts/week | — | 8–15 | 20–40 | 50–100 |
| Trial-to-paid conversion | — | 20% (ramp) | 25% | 30% |
| Paid accounts (cumulative) | — | 10–20 | 40–80 | 200–400 |
| Month MRR | — | $500–$1,500 | $2,000–$5,000 | $12,000–$25,000 |

### MRR Build Model (Month 6 projection)

Assuming:
- 250 paid accounts at month 6
- Mix: 50% Solo, 35% Agencia, 13% Pro, 2% Empresa (conservative)
- Empresa avg: $350/mo

| Tier | Accounts | MRR/account | MRR |
|------|----------|-------------|-----|
| Solo | 125 | $12 | $1,500 |
| Agencia | 87 | $45 | $3,915 |
| Pro | 33 | $120 | $3,960 |
| Empresa | 5 | $350 | $1,750 |
| **Total** | **250** | — | **$11,125** |

**Month-6 MRR target: $10,000–$15,000 USD**  
**Month-12 MRR target: $30,000–$50,000 USD** (post-GA full run rate)

### Conversion Rate Benchmarks (Vertical SaaS)
- Trial-to-paid: 25% is achievable with good onboarding (industry range: 15–40%)
- Waitlist-to-trial: 30–40% (expect drop-off; prioritize quality waitlist over volume)
- Monthly churn target: <4% (Agencia), <3% (Pro), <2% (Empresa)

### North Star Metric
**Monthly Active Paying Accounts (MAPA)** — counts only accounts that logged in AND listed/managed at least 1 property in the past 30 days. Filters out dormant paid accounts that inflate MRR but indicate churn risk.

## Appendix: Channel Attribution Model

For launch, use a simple UTM-based last-touch model:
- `utm_source`: organic, google, meta, linkedin, cucicba, referral, direct
- `utm_medium`: cpc, email, social, partner, referral
- `utm_campaign`: pre-launch, beta, migration, ga

Track in the analytics dashboard by week. Review attribution monthly and reallocate budget from underperforming to overperforming channels. Move to multi-touch attribution model at 500+ paid accounts.
