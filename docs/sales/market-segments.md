# Argentine Real Estate Agency Landscape — Market Segments

> Framing: Corredor's total addressable market for B2B SaaS CRM/listings management.
> Primary geography: Argentina, with CABA and GBA as first beachhead.

---

## 1. Segment Sizing

### Registered Brokers (Corredores Inmobiliarios)

| Registry | Scope | Estimated registered brokers |
|---------|-------|----------------------------|
| **CUCICBA** | Ciudad Autónoma de Buenos Aires (CABA) | ~8,000–10,000 active |
| **CMCPSI** | Provincia de Buenos Aires (GBA + interior) | ~15,000–20,000 |
| **Colegio de Rosario** | Rosario + Gran Rosario | ~3,000–4,000 |
| **Córdoba** | Córdoba province | ~4,000–5,000 |
| **Other provinces** | Rest of AR | ~10,000–15,000 |
| **Total (estimated)** | Argentina | **~40,000–55,000 licensed brokers** |

> Note: Not all registered brokers operate independently or need a CRM. Many work inside larger agencies. The SaaS unit of sale is the **agency**, not the individual broker.

### Agencies (inmobiliarias) as billing units

Argentina has an estimated **12,000–18,000** active real estate agencies operating at any given time. "Agency" ranges from a solo broker with a license to multi-branch operations with 50+ agents.

**Beachhead (Year 1 focus):** CABA + GBA — approximately **4,000–6,000** agencies with consistent transaction volume.

## 2. Agency Breakdown by Size

| Segment | Size (agents) | Estimated # agencies (AR) | % of total agencies | Notes |
|---------|--------------|--------------------------|--------------------|----|
| **Solo broker** | 1 | ~4,000–5,000 | ~30% | License + phone + WhatsApp; minimal tech |
| **Small team** | 2–5 | ~6,000–7,000 | ~40% | Core Tokko customer; manages 30–200 listings |
| **Mid-size** | 6–20 | ~2,000–3,000 | ~20% | Multiple sucursales possible; needs multi-user permissions |
| **Regional chain** | 21–50 | ~400–600 | ~5% | Multi-branch, dedicated admin, portal accounts per branch |
| **Large / national chain** | 50+ | ~100–200 | ~1–2% | RE/MAX, Century 21, Remax franchisees; bespoke contracts |

### ICP focus zones
- **Primary ICP:** Small team (2–5) + Mid-size (6–20) — highest CAC efficiency, clearest pain, most likely to switch
- **Land-and-expand:** Solo brokers — free or low-cost plan, grow with them as they hire
- **Enterprise (later):** Regional chains with multi-branch needs after product matures

## 3. Technology Adoption

Estimates based on public forum behavior, Tokko's market positioning, and industry reports:

| Technology | Estimated adoption (% of agencies) |
|-----------|-----------------------------------|
| Any CRM/software | ~40–50% |
| Tokko Broker specifically | ~30–35% (of all agencies); **~70–80%** of those using any CRM |
| Properati/Navent CRM | ~5–8% |
| Generic tools (HubSpot, spreadsheets, etc.) | ~5–10% |
| No software (phone + paper + WhatsApp groups) | ~50–60% |

**Key insight:** The majority of Argentine agencies use **no dedicated CRM**. This is both a challenge (no switch budget, no concept of SaaS pricing) and an opportunity (no switching cost from a competitor — greenfield acquisition).

### WhatsApp as the dominant channel
- Virtually 100% of Argentine agencies use WhatsApp for client communication
- It is the #1 lead response channel, ahead of phone calls and email
- Tokko has **no native WhatsApp integration** — this is our single biggest product wedge

## 4. Decision Maker Profile

| Agency size | Who decides software purchases? | Budget holder | Sales motion |
|------------|-------------------------------|--------------|-------------|
| Solo broker | The broker themselves | Themselves | Self-serve trial → credit card |
| Small team (2–5) | Agency owner / titular | Owner | Demo → trial → close |
| Mid-size (6–20) | Owner or senior manager | Owner | Demo → proposal → multi-week evaluation |
| Chain (20+) | Operations manager + owner sign-off | Owner/Board | Formal evaluation → legal → contract |

**No IT managers** in typical Argentine real estate agencies — tech decisions are made by agency owners, often non-technical. Implication: UI clarity and "it just works" matter more than feature depth in the early pitch.

**Influencer: the agency's admin / "secretaria"** — the person who actually uses the CRM daily. Getting their buy-in is critical. Demo to them, get them excited, and they'll sell internally.

## 5. Sales Cycle Estimates

| Segment | Average sales cycle | Key stages |
|---------|--------------------|----|
| Solo broker (self-serve) | 1–7 days | Discovers product → tries → pays |
| Small team (2–5) | 2–4 weeks | Demo → trial → owner decision → onboard |
| Mid-size (6–20) | 4–8 weeks | Demo → trial → pricing negotiation → contract |
| Chain (20+) | 8–20 weeks | Formal eval → IT check → legal → onboard |

**AR-specific factors that affect sales cycle:**
- **Peso volatility:** Budget conversations in ARS are complicated by inflation. USD pricing simplifies mental accounting for owners but creates friction at checkout (many don't have USD payment methods). Offer both.
- **Holiday rhythm:** January is dead (everyone is in Mar del Plata or Bariloche). Productivity peaks Feb–June and Sep–Nov.
- **End-of-year urgency:** December closes fast — agency owners want to start fresh in January, which creates a close window in November.
- **Decision by feel:** Argentine SMB owners make decisions relationally. Referrals and demos in person or by video convert better than email sequences.

## 6. Market Opportunity Sizing

### Conservative TAM (Year 1 beachhead: CABA + GBA)
- ~5,000 agencies in beachhead
- ~40% have budget for software → ~2,000 addressable
- Average ACV (Annual Contract Value) target: USD 1,200/year (USD 100/month, small team plan)
- **Beachhead TAM: ~USD 2.4M ARR**

### Full Argentine market
- ~15,000 agencies nationally with software budget propensity
- At USD 100/month average: **~USD 18M ARR SAM**

### Expansion (LATAM)
- Similar dynamics in Uruguay, Paraguay, Bolivia, Ecuador (Tokko not dominant there)
- Brazil is a separate market (Loft, Zap/Viva Real dominate) — distinct product localization needed
- **LATAM SAM (ex-BR):** ~USD 60–100M ARR

---

## 7. Recommended Beachhead Strategy

1. **Start in CABA.** Highest agency density, most tech-forward agencies, physical proximity for in-person demos.
2. **Target the frustrated mid-Tokko user.** 3–10 agent agencies who have been on Tokko 2+ years and complain about mobile, WhatsApp, or price increases.
3. **Use chambers as distribution.** A CUCICBA event sponsorship or CMCPSI presence puts you in front of 200+ agencies at once.
4. **Referral program from day 1.** Argentine real estate is a small world. One happy agency = 3 referrals.
5. **Land solo brokers cheap, expand as they grow.** A solo broker today may have a 5-person team in 2 years.
